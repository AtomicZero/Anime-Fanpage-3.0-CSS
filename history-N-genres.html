History:

Since the early days of hitting the scene back in the 70s/80s, anime has departed from it experimental and exploratory phases to becoming a massive and mainstream establishment spawning several iconic world-renowned franchises and possessing a lot of sub-genres. Personally I loved when anime was new an experimental, it was a lot more thought-provoking and touched on a lot of topics that were deeply philosophical or even taboo [at the time of the anime release].

Genres:

The four main sub-genres of anime are based on the general ages of their intended audience:

  1. Shounen  [young boys]
  2. Shoujo  [young girls]
  3. Seinen  [older boys]
  4. Josei  [older girls]

Then these sub-genres are further cataloged by the usual entertainment categories: sci-fi, comedy, action, horror, romance, historic, fantasy, etc. There are some sub-categories unique to anime, however, such as: Mecha [giant robots] and Sentai [colour-coded heroes/squads] or those of a more mature level like, shounen-ai or ecchi
